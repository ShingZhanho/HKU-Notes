\question
Let \( z = x + yi \). Compute \(\text{Im}(e^{z^2})\) as an elementary function of \(x\) and \(y\).

\begin{solution}
    Evaluate $z^2$ first:
    \[
        z^2 = (x + yi)^2 = x^2 + 2xyi + (yi)^2 = (x^2 - y^2) + 2xyi
    \]
    Then:
    \begin{align*}
        e^{z^2} &= e^{(x^2 - y^2) + 2xyi} \\
        &= e^{x^2 - y^2} \cdot e^{2xyi} \\
        &= e^{x^2 - y^2} \left( \cos(2xy) + i\sin(2xy) \right) \\
        &= e^{x^2 - y^2} \cos(2xy) + i e^{x^2 - y^2} \sin(2xy)
    \end{align*}

    Thus, the imaginary part is:
    \[
        \text{Im}(e^{z^2}) = \boxed{e^{x^2 - y^2} \sin(2xy)}
    \]
\end{solution}
