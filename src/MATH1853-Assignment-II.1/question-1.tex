\question
For $z \neq 0$ and $\text{Arg}(z) \in (-\pi, \pi]$, the principal value of $\log z$ is defined by  
\[
\log(z) = \ln|z| + i \, \text{Arg}(z).
\]  
Compute $\log(z)$ for $z = \displaystyle\frac{1 + i}{1 - i}$.

\begin{solution}
    Simplify $z$:
    \begin{equation*}
        z = \frac{1 + i}{1 - i} \cdot \frac{1 + i}{1 + i} = \frac{(1 + i)^2}{1 - i^2} = \frac{1 + 2i + i^2}{1 + 1} = \frac{2i}{2} = i.
    \end{equation*}
    Then, $|z| = \sqrt{(0)^2+(1)^2} = 1$, and $\text{Arg}(z) = \displaystyle\frac{\pi}{2}$ since $z$ lies on the positive imaginary axis.

    By definition, $\displaystyle\log(z) = \ln 1 + i \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} = 0 + i \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} = \boxed{\frac{\pi}{2} i}$.
\end{solution}
