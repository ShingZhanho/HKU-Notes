\startQwTags{2024}{May}{Q33}{\tgCE \tgCEOnesComplement \tgCETwosComplement}
\begin{solution}
    First, we convert $-107$ and $-21$ to their 8-bit one's complement representations:
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{rccccccccl}
            $\mathrm{NOT})$  & 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 1 & (+107 in binary)\\\hline
                             & 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & ($-107$)\\
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{rccccccccl}
            $\mathrm{NOT})$  & 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & (+21 in binary)\\\hline
                             & 1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & ($-21$)\\
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}

    Then, we add the two numbers together:
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{rccccccccl}
                             & 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & ($-107$)\\
            $+)$             & 1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & ($-21$)\\\hline
                $\cancel{1}$ & 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & \\
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}

    Since carry bit is generated, we need to add it back to the result:
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{rccccccccl}
                             & 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & \\
            $+)$             &   &   &   &   &   &   &   & 1 & \\\hline
                             & 0 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & \\
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    
    The result has the most significant bit being 0, which indicates that the result is positive.
    However, $(-107)+(-21)<0$, so the result is incorrect.

    If we use two's complement representation, we have:
    \begin{center}
        \begin{tabular}{rccccccccl}
                             & 1 & 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & ($-107$)\\
            $+)$             & 1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 1 & ($-21$)\\\hline
                $\cancel{1}$ & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & ($-128$)\\
        \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    We can see that the result is correct.
\end{solution}
