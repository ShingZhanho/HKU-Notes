        \item Light is emitted when electrons falls from the conduction band to the valence band (recombination with holes), releasing energy in the form of photons. Incorrect.

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q9}{\tgElectronics \tgElectronicsMultiplexing}
    \mcqAns{C}

    In frequency-division multiplexing (FDM) and wavelength-division multiplexing (WDM), signals are joint together and transmitted simultaneously over a shared bandwidth.
    At the receiver end, the signals need to be separated by the bandpass filters.
    Therefore, (i) and (iii) need bandpass filters.

    In time-devision multiplexing (TDM), signals are transmitted in different time slots, one at a time.
    Therefore, the signals received at a given time slot only contains one signal, no bandpass filters are required.

    Hence, C is correct.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q25}{\tgEE \tgEEPowerSystems \tgEERMS}
    \mcqAns{A}
    
    The voltage $v(t)$ for one period is given by:
    \[
        v(t) = \begin{cases}
            2, & t\in[0, 0.5T) \\
            \frac{-4t}{T}+4, & t\in[0.5T, T)
        \end{cases}.
    \]
    The RMS voltage value is given by:
    \begin{align*}
        V_{\mathrm{RMS}} &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_0^{T} v^2(t) \d t} \\
        &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\left[\int_0^{0.5T} 4 \d t + \int_{0.5T}^{T} \left(\frac{-4t}{T}+4\right)^2 \d t\right]} \\
        &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\left(2T+\frac{2}{3}T\right)} = \sqrt{\frac{8}{3}}\,\mathrm{V} \approx 1.63 \,\mathrm{V}
    \end{align*}
\end{qsolution}
