\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q1}{\tgElectronics \tgElectronicsSemiconductor}
    \mcqAns{A}

    Holes are defined as the absence of electrons in the valence band, they cannot ``move'' to the conduction band.
    Therefore, D and E are incorrect.
    Electrons cannot exist in the band gap, so C is incorrect.
    Electrons have more energy in the conduction band than in the valence band, so A is correct.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q2}{\tgElectronics \tgElectronicsSemiconductor}
    \mcqAns{C}

    In a p-type (``p'' stands for ``positive'') semiconductor, the majority carriers are holes.
    Therefore, A and B are incorrect.
    Since holes are the majority carriers, there are more holes than electrons, so C is correct and D is incorrect.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q3}{\tgElectronics \tgElectronicsDiodes}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q7}{\tgElectronics \tgElectronicsOptoelectronics}
    \mcqAns{C}

    Explanation:
    \begin{enumroman}
        \item The colour of light in LEDs is determined by the band gap of the semiconductor. Correct.
        \item An LED emits light when electrons are interjecting from the conduction band to the valence band, which only happens when forward-biased. Correct.
        \item Light is emitted when electrons falls from the conduction band to the valence band (recombination with holes), releasing energy in the form of photons. Incorrect.
        \item Same as (iii). Correct.
    \end{enumroman}
    Therefore, (i), (ii), and (iv) are correct, so C is correct.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q8}{\tgElectronics \tgElectronicsFM}
    \mcqAns{A}

    In FM, frequency deviation measures how much the frequency of the modulated signal deviates from the carrier frequency.
    The frequency deviation is directly proportional to the amplitude of the modulating signal.
    Therefore, maximum frequency deviation occurs at the maximum intensity of the modulating signal.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q9}{\tgElectronics \tgElectronicsMultiplexing}
    \mcqAns{C}

    In frequency-division multiplexing (FDM) and wavelength-division multiplexing (WDM), signals are joint together and transmitted simultaneously over a shared bandwidth.
    At the receiver end, the signals need to be separated by the bandpass filters.
    Therefore, (i) and (iii) need bandpass filters.

    In time-devision multiplexing (TDM), signals are transmitted in different time slots, one at a time.
    Therefore, the signals received at a given time slot only contains one signal, no bandpass filters are required.

    Hence, C is correct.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q18}{\tgCE \tgCEComputerOrganisation \tgAI}
    \mcqAns{A}

    The Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) is responsible for arithmetic and logic operations.
    Typical functions include bitwise operations (\texttt{AND}, \texttt{OR}, \texttt{XOR}, etc.) and logical comparisons (equal, less than, etc.).
    Determining memory access timing or resetting the datapath are tasks of the control unit, not the ALU.
    Hence only (i) and (ii) are correct, therefore A.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q24}{\tgEE \tgEEThreePhase}
    \mcqAns{D}
    
    Consider the phase diagram for voltages:
    \figHere{ThreePhase-Y-Voltage-Phasor.pdf}{width=0.8\textwidth}
    The line voltage $V_{ab}$ is given by:
    \begin{align*}
        V_{ab}&=V_{an}-V_{bn}=V_{an}+V_{nb}\\
        &=V_{ph}+V_{ph}\angle 120^\circ \\
        &=V_{ph}\left(0+1\mathrm{j}\right) + V_{ph}\left(-\frac{1}{2}+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\mathrm{j}\right) \\
        &=\sqrt{3}V_{ph}\angle 30^\circ
    \end{align*}
    Note that counterclockwise is defined as positive.
    Therefore, line voltage is $\sqrt{3}$ times the phase voltage, and leads the phase voltage by $30^\circ$.
\end{qsolution}

\begin{qsolution}{2024}{Dec}{Q25}{\tgEE \tgEEPowerSystems \tgEERMS}
    \mcqAns{A}
    
    The voltage $v(t)$ for one period is given by:
    \[
        v(t) = \begin{cases}
            2, & t\in[0, 0.5T) \\
            \frac{-4t}{T}+4, & t\in[0.5T, T)
        \end{cases}.
    \]
    The RMS voltage value is given by:
    \begin{align*}
        V_{\mathrm{RMS}} &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_0^{T} v^2(t) \d t} \\
        &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\left[\int_0^{0.5T} 4 \d t + \int_{0.5T}^{T} \left(\frac{-4t}{T}+4\right)^2 \d t\right]} \\
        &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\left(2T+\frac{2}{3}T\right)} = \sqrt{\frac{8}{3}}\,\mathrm{V} \approx 1.63 \,\mathrm{V}
    \end{align*}
\end{qsolution}
